Can Default Bail Be Denied After the Charge Sheet is Filed?

By Abhayjeet Singh Rao5 August 20264 min read

The critical question is: which was filed first — the bail application or the charge sheet?

Scenario 1: Charge Sheet Filed First

If the prosecution files the charge sheet before the accused files the default bail application — even if it is the 60th or 90th day — the right to default bail is extinguished. The accused can still apply for regular bail on merits, but the automatic, indefeasible right of default bail is no longer available.

Scenario 2: Bail Application Filed First

If the accused files the default bail application before the charge sheet is filed, the right has been exercised and cannot be defeated by the subsequent filing of the charge sheet. The Supreme Court in M. Ravindran v. Intelligence Officer, DRI (2021) was emphatic on this point: once the right is exercised, it cannot be taken away.

Scenario 3: Both Filed on the Same Day

This is the most contested situation. Courts examine the exact time of filing. If the bail application was filed at 10 AM and the charge sheet at 2 PM, the accused's right is preserved. Some courts maintain time-stamped records; in others, the accused may need to prove the sequence through court diary entries or filing receipts.

Can an Incomplete Charge Sheet Defeat Default Bail?

The prosecution sometimes files a skeletal or incomplete charge sheet to defeat the right. Courts have held that a charge sheet must be substantive — it must contain the essential material to sustain the prosecution. A bare-bones filing lacking witness statements, FSL reports, or other essential documents may be challenged as not being a valid charge sheet.

What About Supplementary Charge Sheets?

The filing of a supplementary charge sheet does not restart the clock. If the main charge sheet was filed within time but the supplementary charge sheet is filed later, the accused cannot claim default bail based on the delay in the supplementary charge sheet. Conversely, if the main charge sheet was not filed in time, filing a supplementary charge sheet later does not cure the defect.

Practical Advice

There is no room for delay. On the day the 60th or 90th day expires, file the default bail application first thing in the morning. Every hour matters. Have the application drafted and ready to file before the date arrives. Brief your surety in advance so that bail bonds can be furnished immediately upon grant.
