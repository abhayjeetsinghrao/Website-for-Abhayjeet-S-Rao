Can a Husband Claim Maintenance in India?

By Abhayjeet Singh Rao10 July 20264 min read

The question of whether a husband can claim maintenance from his wife arises with increasing frequency as dual-income households become the norm and the earning differential between spouses sometimes favours the wife.

The Short Answer

Yes, under the Hindu Marriage Act (HMA) — Sections 24 and 25 are gender-neutral and allow either spouse to claim maintenance. No, under Section 125 CrPC / Section 144 BNSS — this provision is only available to the wife, children, and parents.

Maintenance Under the Hindu Marriage Act

Section 24: Interim Maintenance

Section 24 HMA provides that where either the husband or the wife has no independent income sufficient for their support, the court may order the other spouse to pay monthly maintenance during the pendency of the proceedings. The language is explicitly gender-neutral: "either the wife or the husband."

Section 25: Permanent Alimony

Section 25 HMA similarly allows either spouse to claim permanent alimony or maintenance at the time of, or subsequent to, the decree of divorce. The court considers:

  • The respondent's own income and property
  • The income and property of the applicant
  • The conduct of the parties
  • Other circumstances of the case

When Courts Have Granted Maintenance to Husbands

While relatively rare, courts have awarded maintenance to husbands in cases where:

  • The husband was physically disabled or suffering from a chronic illness rendering him unable to work
  • The wife was substantially wealthier — e.g., a successful professional or business owner while the husband was unemployed
  • The husband had sacrificed his career to support the wife's professional advancement (though this argument is more common in Western jurisdictions)

The Practical Reality

Despite the gender-neutral language of the HMA, courts in India continue to apply maintenance provisions predominantly in favour of wives. This reflects the socio-economic reality that, on average, wives are still more likely to be financially dependent. A husband claiming maintenance faces a higher evidentiary burden and must clearly demonstrate financial inability to support himself.

Section 125 CrPC: Only for Wives

Section 125 CrPC (now Section 144 BNSS) provides a quick maintenance remedy, but it is available only to the wife, children, and parents — not to the husband. This means a husband seeking maintenance must proceed under the HMA (Sections 24/25), which is a slower process tied to the divorce proceedings.

Practical Takeaway

If you are a husband seeking maintenance, the legal avenue exists under the HMA, but success depends on demonstrating genuine financial need and the wife's ability to pay. Document your financial position thoroughly — bank statements, medical records (if claiming inability to work), and evidence of the wife's income and assets.
