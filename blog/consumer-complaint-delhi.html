How to File a Consumer Complaint in Delhi (2026)

By Abhayjeet Singh Rao27 July 20268 min read

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 provides a fast-track mechanism for resolving disputes between consumers and sellers, manufacturers, or service providers. If you have been sold defective goods, received deficient service, or been subjected to unfair trade practices, you can file a complaint before the Consumer Dispute Redressal Commission.

Who is a Consumer?

Any person who buys goods or hires services for a consideration (including online and e-commerce purchases). The Act excludes persons who buy goods for resale or commercial purposes. However, if goods are bought for earning a livelihood through self-employment, the buyer is still a consumer.

Grounds for Filing

  • Defective goods: Goods that do not conform to the description, are not fit for purpose, or have manufacturing defects
  • Deficiency in service: Any fault, imperfection, or inadequacy in the quality, nature, or manner of service — including delays, negligence, and non-performance
  • Unfair trade practice: Misleading advertisements, false representation, or deceptive practices
  • Restrictive trade practice: Unreasonable restrictions on the consumer's use of the goods or services
  • Overcharging: Charging more than the MRP or the agreed price

Pecuniary Jurisdiction

  • District Commission: Claims up to INR 50 lakhs
  • State Commission: Claims from INR 50 lakhs to INR 2 crores
  • National Commission (NCDRC): Claims above INR 2 crores

Territorial Jurisdiction

The complaint may be filed where:

  • The opposite party (seller/service provider) resides or carries on business
  • The branch office of the opposite party is located (where the cause of action arose)
  • The complainant resides or personally works for gain (added by the 2019 Act — a significant pro-consumer change)

Step-by-Step Filing Process

  1. Send a written notice to the seller/service provider demanding relief (not mandatory but strengthens your case)
  2. Draft the complaint — setting out the facts, the deficiency or defect, the relief sought, and the compensation claimed
  3. Compile documents: Invoice, payment receipt, warranty card, photographs of defective goods, correspondence (emails, WhatsApp messages), the notice sent and any reply received
  4. File the complaint — either physically at the commission or online through the e-daakhil portal (edaakhil.nic.in)
  5. Pay the prescribed fee — based on the value of goods/services (nominal — ranges from a few hundred to a few thousand rupees)
  6. Attend hearings — the commission issues notice to the opposite party, who files a reply. Both sides present evidence and arguments.
  7. Order — the commission passes an order, typically within 3-5 months for straightforward cases

Remedies Available

  • Replacement of defective goods or re-performance of deficient service
  • Refund of the price paid, with interest
  • Compensation for loss or injury suffered
  • Punitive damages in cases of particularly egregious conduct
  • Discontinuance of the unfair trade practice
  • Costs of the proceedings

Practical Advice

  • Preserve all evidence — keep the defective product, take photographs and videos, save all communications
  • File within 2 years — the limitation period is strict
  • You can appear in person — a lawyer is not mandatory before consumer commissions, though professional assistance helps in complex cases
  • E-commerce purchases are covered — the 2019 Act specifically covers online purchases and makes the e-commerce platform jointly liable
