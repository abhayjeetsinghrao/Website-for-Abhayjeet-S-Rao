Pecuniary Jurisdiction of Consumer Forums in India

By Abhayjeet Singh Rao29 July 20263 min read

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 establishes a three-tier dispute resolution mechanism. Which forum you approach depends on the total value of goods purchased or services hired, plus the compensation claimed.

The Three Tiers

  • District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (District Commission): Claims up to INR 50 lakhs
  • State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (State Commission): Claims from INR 50 lakhs to INR 2 crores
  • National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (NCDRC): Claims above INR 2 crores

How to Calculate the Value

The value for determining jurisdiction is the total value of goods or services, plus compensation claimed. For example:

  • You bought a car for INR 15 lakhs and claim INR 5 lakhs in compensation = INR 20 lakhs → District Commission
  • You bought a flat for INR 80 lakhs and claim a refund = INR 80 lakhs → State Commission
  • You seek a refund of INR 1.5 crores plus INR 75 lakhs compensation = INR 2.25 crores → NCDRC

Appeals

  • Appeal from District Commission → State Commission (within 45 days)
  • Appeal from State Commission → NCDRC (within 30 days)
  • Appeal from NCDRC → Supreme Court (within 30 days)

Delhi-Specific Information

In Delhi, there are District Consumer Commissions at each district. The State Commission is located at the Delhi Judicial Academy complex. For claims above INR 2 crores, the NCDRC is located in New Delhi.

Common Mistake

Filing before the wrong forum is a common error that wastes time. If you file a claim worth INR 60 lakhs before the District Commission, it will be returned as beyond its jurisdiction. Always calculate the total claim value carefully before filing.
