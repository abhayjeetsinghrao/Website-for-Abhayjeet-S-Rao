What is the Time Limit for Filing Section 34 of the Arbitration Act?

By Abhayjeet Singh Rao26 August 20263 min read

The time limit for filing an application under Section 34 of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act is 3 months from the date of receipt of the arbitral award. The court may grant an extension of up to 30 days if the applicant shows sufficient cause. No further extension is possible — the 3+1 month limit is absolute.

When Does the Clock Start?

The 3-month period begins from the date the party receives the arbitral award. If the award is sent by post, the date of receipt (not the date of dispatch) is the starting point. If the award is delivered in person at the hearing, the date of the hearing is the starting point.

If a party requests correction or interpretation of the award under Section 33, the 3-month period runs from the date of receipt of the corrected/interpreted award.

The 30-Day Extension

Section 34(3) allows the court to condone delay up to 30 days beyond the 3-month period if "sufficient cause" is shown. This is discretionary — the court must be satisfied that the delay was genuine and not due to negligence. Common grounds include:

  • Illness of the party or their lawyer
  • Award received during a court vacation or holiday
  • Delay caused by obtaining certified copies

However, routine delays — "lawyer was busy," "client was travelling," "needed time to take instructions" — are generally not condoned.

No Condonation Beyond 3+1 Months

The Supreme Court in Union of India v. Popular Construction Co. (2001) held that Section 34(3) is a special limitation provision that excludes the application of Section 5 of the Limitation Act (general power to condone delay). This means:

  • No Section 34 application can be entertained after 3 months + 30 days
  • The court has no discretion to condone delay beyond this period, regardless of the reasons
  • This is one of the strictest limitation provisions in Indian law

Practical Advice

If you receive an adverse arbitral award, begin preparing the Section 34 application immediately. Do not wait for the limitation period to approach. The application requires careful analysis of the award, identification of the specific grounds under Section 34(2), and compilation of supporting material. Three months may seem generous, but it passes quickly — especially if the award is complex and involves voluminous records.
