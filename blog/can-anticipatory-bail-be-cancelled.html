Can Anticipatory Bail Be Cancelled?

By Abhayjeet Singh Rao9 June 20265 min read

Yes, anticipatory bail can be cancelled. While the grant of anticipatory bail protects an accused from arrest, this protection is not absolute or irrevocable. The prosecution or the complainant may apply for cancellation if circumstances warrant it, and the court has the discretion to withdraw the protection it previously granted.

Grounds for Cancellation

Courts have identified several grounds on which anticipatory bail may be cancelled:

  • Misuse of liberty — If the accused uses the protection to threaten witnesses, tamper with evidence, or influence the investigation, the court may cancel bail.
  • Non-cooperation with investigation — Anticipatory bail is granted on the implicit or explicit condition that the accused will cooperate with the investigating officer. Failure to appear when summoned or refusal to answer questions can lead to cancellation.
  • New facts or supervening circumstances — If material facts emerge after the grant of bail that were not before the court — such as recovery of incriminating evidence, identification by witnesses, or discovery of the accused's role in a larger conspiracy — the court may reconsider.
  • Flight risk — If the accused attempts to flee the jurisdiction, applies for a passport without permission, or makes preparations to abscond.
  • Violation of bail conditions — Breach of any condition imposed by the court — such as leaving the country, failing to mark attendance, or contacting witnesses — is a ground for cancellation.
  • Suppression of material facts — If the court finds that the accused obtained anticipatory bail by concealing relevant facts or making false statements, it may cancel the bail on the ground of fraud upon the court.

The Legal Test

The Supreme Court has held that the power to cancel bail must be exercised with care. In Dataram Singh v. State of Uttar Pradesh (2018), the Court reiterated that cancellation requires "supervening circumstances" or a showing that the accused has misused the bail or that the fair trial is being prejudiced. A mere change of mind by the prosecution is not sufficient.

The distinction between rejection of bail and cancellation of bail is important. Cancellation of bail already granted is not the same as rejection of bail in the first instance. The court must find that the accused has misconducted themselves or that new circumstances have arisen.

Which Court Can Cancel Anticipatory Bail?

An application for cancellation may be filed before:

  • The same court that granted the anticipatory bail (Sessions Court or High Court)
  • A higher court — if the Sessions Court granted bail, the State may apply for cancellation before the High Court; if the High Court granted bail, the State may approach the Supreme Court

Procedure for Cancellation

  1. The prosecution or complainant files an application setting out the grounds for cancellation, supported by affidavit and documentary evidence.
  2. The court issues notice to the accused and fixes a date for hearing.
  3. Both sides are heard. The accused may file a reply affidavit.
  4. The court passes an order either cancelling the anticipatory bail or dismissing the application.
  5. If bail is cancelled, the accused loses the protection against arrest and may be taken into custody. They would then need to apply for regular bail.

What Happens After Cancellation?

Once anticipatory bail is cancelled, the accused is in the same position as a person against whom a non-bailable warrant has been issued. The police may arrest them, and they would need to apply for regular bail from custody. The fact that anticipatory bail was earlier granted and then cancelled is a factor that the court may consider — often adversely — while deciding the regular bail application.

Practical Advice

If you have been granted anticipatory bail, strict compliance with the conditions imposed is essential. The most common reason for cancellation in practice is non-cooperation with the investigation — failing to appear before the IO, not providing documents, or evading custodial questioning. Courts take a dim view of accused persons who secure anticipatory bail and then treat it as a shield to avoid investigation altogether.

If you learn that a cancellation application has been filed, engage counsel immediately. The hearing is adversarial and the stakes are high — cancellation leads directly to loss of liberty.
