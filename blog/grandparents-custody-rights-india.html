Can Grandparents Get Custody of a Child in India?

By Abhayjeet Singh Rao24 July 20264 min read

Yes, grandparents can obtain custody of a grandchild in India, though the path is more difficult than for parents. Indian law does not give grandparents automatic custody rights, but the Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 allows any person — including grandparents — to apply for guardianship if the welfare of the child demands it.

When Grandparents May Get Custody

  • Both parents deceased: The most straightforward case. Grandparents are often the natural choice.
  • Both parents unfit: Substance abuse, domestic violence, neglect, criminal conduct, or mental illness rendering both parents incapable.
  • One parent deceased, surviving parent unfit: If the surviving parent cannot care for the child, grandparents may apply.
  • Abandonment: If both parents have abandoned the child and the grandparents have been the de facto caregivers.
  • Child already living with grandparents: If the child has been living with grandparents for a significant period and is settled, courts are reluctant to disrupt the status quo.

The Legal Process

  1. File a petition under the Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 before the District Court having jurisdiction
  2. The court issues notice to the parents (if alive) and any other interested party
  3. A welfare inquiry is conducted — a social worker visits the grandparents' home and the parents' home (if applicable) and files a report
  4. The court considers the welfare of the child as the paramount factor and passes an order

Key Considerations

  • Age and health of grandparents: Courts consider whether the grandparents are physically capable of raising the child for the foreseeable future
  • Financial stability: The ability to provide for the child's needs
  • Emotional bond: Evidence of a close and nurturing relationship between the grandparents and the child
  • Child's wishes: If the child is old enough, their preference for living with grandparents is given weight
  • Parental consent: If the parents consent to the grandparents having custody, the process is simpler

Grandparents' Visitation Rights

Even without custody, grandparents may apply for visitation rights. While there is no explicit statutory provision for grandparental visitation, courts have increasingly recognised the importance of the grandparent-grandchild relationship. The Supreme Court has observed that maintaining ties with extended family is in the child's welfare.

Practical Advice

If you are a grandparent seeking custody, document the reasons why the parents are unable to care for the child, and demonstrate your capacity to provide a stable and nurturing environment. If the child has been living with you, obtain school records, medical records, and other documentation showing your role as the primary caregiver. Courts prioritise continuity and stability in the child's life.
